Research Study: How Alarm Clocks Influence Sleep and Physiology

This study investigates the physiological effects of waking up with an alarm compared to waking naturally. Over a two-week period, participants tracked heart rate, core body temperature, and vigilance using an Apple Watch, an oral thermometer, and the "Lights Out" reaction time app.

Key Findings

  • Heart Rate: Waking up with an alarm resulted in a higher heart rate compared to waking naturally.
  • Core Body Temperature: Lower upon waking with an alarm, likely due to disrupted sleep cycles.
  • Reaction Time: Slower reaction times were observed when waking with an alarm.

Conclusions

The data suggests that waking with an alarm negatively affects physiological responses, potentially impacting morning alertness and cognitive performance. The increase in heart rate may be due to waking during REM sleep, while the lower core body temperature suggests the body hasn't fully transitioned to wakefulness. Slower reaction times further indicate impaired cognitive function immediately after waking with an alarm.

Note on This Research

This research was conducted as part of my undergraduate studies. While I have taken great care in the methodology and analysis, I am still learning and growing as a researcher. Future refinements and additional studies may provide even more insights into the effects of alarm-induced waking.
