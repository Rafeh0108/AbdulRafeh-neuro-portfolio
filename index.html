About Me

Hi, I'm AbdulRafeh Usmani, a recent Biomedical Science graduate with a deep interest in neuroscience research. I am passionate about exploring how the brain works, from circadian rhythms to neuroplasticity. My hands-on experience with neurodivergent populations and laboratory techniques, along with my coding skills in R and web development, drive my curiosity to bridge the gap between data and understanding the human mind.

I'm currently expanding my coding skills to create interactive tools and visualizations that can make complex neuroscience concepts more accessible. Whether through data analysis in R, designing scientific illustrations with BioRender, or building creative web experiences, I love blending science and technology to tell impactful stories.

How Alarm Clocks Influence Heart Rate, Core Body Temperature, and Vigilance Compared to Free Days

This research explores the effects of alarm-induced waking on physiological parameters such as heart rate, core body temperature, and vigilance. Findings indicate significant variations on alarm-clock days compared to free days.
